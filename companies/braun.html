About

Braun was founded in Frankfurt in 1921 by Max Braun as a radio and electrical components manufacturer. After Max's death in 1951, his sons Artur and Erwin took over and made the pivotal decision to transform Braun from a manufacturer into a design-driven brand — hiring a team from the Hochschule für Gestaltung Ulm (HfG Ulm) and appointing Dieter Rams as head of design in 1961.

Through the late 1950s and 1960s, Braun became the defining example of the Deutscher Werkbund ideal applied to mass-produced consumer goods: functional, honest, geometric, and meticulously detailed. The visual coherence across product lines — radios, shavers, kitchen appliances, hi-fi equipment — was unprecedented for a consumer brand at the time.

Braun's design philosophy was codified through:

  • Close collaboration with HfG Ulm designers (Gugelot, Aicher, Bill)
  • Dieter Rams's Ten Principles of Good Design
  • A strict house palette: white, grey, black, and occasional primary accents
  • Standardized grid systems and typographic consistency across all products

Braun was acquired by Gillette in 1967 and later by Procter & Gamble in 2005. The design-led era under Rams is considered its creative peak and remains one of the most influential corporate design programs in history.
